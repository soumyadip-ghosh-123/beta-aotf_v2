# The Provider Workflow

For providers (teachers and freelancers), the platform functions as a streamlined job board with transparent tracking.

## 1. Onboarding & Payment

Providers must register and pay a one-time fee before they can see or apply to any posts.

- The provider signs up (Clerk).
- They hit the Onboarding Gate (`proxy.ts`).
- They select a plan (`teacher` or `teacher_candidate`).
- They pay via Razorpay.
- The system unlocks their dashboard.

## 2. Profile Setup

A complete profile is crucial. When an admin reviews an application, the provider's profile data (experience, qualifications, subjects) is heavily factored into the decision.

Providers upload an avatar (Cloudinary), fill out their teaching/job experience, and list their subjects.

## 3. Browsing & Applying

Providers visit the **Tuitions** (`/posts`) or **Jobs** (`/jobs`) boards.

- They can filter by location (online/offline), subjects, or text search.
- When they find a suitable post, they click "Apply".
- They submit a brief Cover Letter explaining why they are a good fit.

**Rule**: A provider can only apply to a post *once*. The system enforces this at the database level with a compound unique index.

## 4. Tracking Application Status

Unlike traditional job boards where applications go into a black hole, AOTF provides real-time status updates.

In their Dashboard, providers see:
- `Applied`: Application submitted.
- `DC Scheduled`: An admin wants to talk to them.
- `Rejected`: Application declined (with a specific reason).

If another provider is selected, their status automatically changes to `Declined` (via the auto-decline cascade), so they know the role is filled and can move on.

## 5. Feedback & Support

If a provider has an issue (e.g., a student didn't pay them, or an admin hasn't responded), they can submit a **Feedback / Report** ticket from their dashboard.

This creates a `Feedback` record that admins see and manage (with its own status lifecycle).
